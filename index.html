GitKraken

GitKraken is a popular graphical user interface (GUI) Git client designed to simplify and streamline the process of working with Git repositories. JSFiddle

JSFiddle is an online web development tool that allows users to write, test, and share HTML, CSS, and JavaScript code snippets in an interactive environment. It provides a simple and intuitive interface where developers can quickly prototype ideas, experiment with code, and collaborate with others.

Replit

Replit is an online platform that provides a browser-based integrated development environment (IDE) for coding in various programming languages. It allows users to write, run, and collaborate on code directly from their web browser without the need for any installations or setup.

Behance

Behance is an online platform owned by Adobe that enables creative professionals to showcase their work, connect with other creatives, and discover new opportunities. It serves as a portfolio platform for designers, artists, photographers, illustrators, and other creative individuals to display their projects and portfolios in a visually appealing and professional manner.

StackExchange

Stack Exchange is a network of question-and-answer websites covering a wide range of topics, from programming and technology to hobbies, science, and more. Each website within the Stack Exchange network is dedicated to a specific topic or subject area and operates as a community-driven platform where users can ask questions, provide answers, and participate in discussions.

Dribbble

Dribbble is a social networking platform and community specifically designed for designers, artists, illustrators, and other creative professionals to showcase their work, share ideas, and connect with others in the design industry. It's a place where designers can upload screenshots or small snippets of their projects, known as "shots", which can include anything from UI/UX designs, illustrations, animations, to graphic designs and more.

Hacker News

Hacker News is a social news website and online community focused on technology, entrepreneurship, startups, computer science, and related topics. It was created by the startup incubator Y Combinator (founded by Paul Graham, Jessica Livingston, Robert Morris, and Trevor Blackwell) in 2007 as a platform for sharing and discussing interesting and relevant news and articles within the tech community.

Stack Overflow

Stack Overflow is a question and answer (Q&A) website primarily focused on programming and software development topics. It was created in 2008 by Jeff Atwood and Joel Spolsky as a platform where developers could ask technical questions, provide answers, and share knowledge with one another.

Medium

Medium is an online publishing platform that allows users to read, write, and interact with articles and stories covering a wide range of topics. Launched in 2012 by Ev Williams, one of the co-founders of Twitter, Medium has grown into a popular destination for both writers and readers seeking high-quality, long-form content.

DEV.to

Dev.to is a community-driven platform aimed at developers and technology enthusiasts. It serves as a space for developers to share knowledge, insights, and experiences related to programming, software development, and technology in general. CodePen is an online community and development environment for front-end web designers and developers. It provides a platform where users can write and share HTML, CSS, and JavaScript code snippets, as well as explore and remix creations from other users.

GitLab

GitLab is a web-based Git repository management service that offers a platform for hosting code repositories, conducting code reviews, tracking issues, and managing projects. It enables development teams to collaborate on software projects and provides a suite of tools to manage the entire lifecycle of software development.

Quora

Quora is a question-and-answer website where users can ask questions on various topics and receive answers from other users. It covers a wide range of subjects, including technology, science, education, business, health, and more. Users can follow topics, browse questions, provide answers, and interact with other users through comments and upvotes. Quora aims to create a platform for knowledge sharing and community engagement by connecting people with diverse expertise and perspectives.

Bitbucket

Bitbucket is a web-based code hosting service that specializes in using Git and Mercurial version control systems to manage code repositories. It allows development teams to store, share, and manage their source code in the cloud and provides a range of tools to support team collaboration and the entire software development lifecycle.

SourceForge

SourceForge is a web-based platform that offers services related to software development. It is primarily known for providing a centralized location for hosting and distributing open-source software projects. SourceForge provides various features and tools to assist developers in managing their projects, collaborating with other developers, and distributing their software to users.

Zhihu

Zhihu (知乎) is a Chinese question-and-answer website similar to Quora. It allows users to ask questions on various topics and receive answers from other users. Zhihu covers a wide range of subjects, including technology, culture, science, education, and more. Users can follow topics, browse questions, provide answers, and interact with other users through comments and votes. Zhihu also features articles, discussions, and live broadcasts on different topics. It serves as a platform for knowledge sharing, community engagement, and intellectual discussion among Chinese-speaking users.

GitBook

GitBook is a modern documentation platform where teams can create, share, and collaborate on documentation for their projects. It allows users to write and publish documentation using a simple and intuitive interface, while also providing powerful features for customization, collaboration, and integration.
